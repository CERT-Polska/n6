# Bots
Category `bots` refers to machines that are infected by malicious software. Usually attacker has full control of infected system. An IP address associated with name below means high probability of infection. 


### android.badbox2
android.badbox2 refers to the BadBox 2.0 operation, an evolution of the BadBox malware campaign found pre-installed on low-cost Android consumer devices (such as TV boxes and tablets). It functions as a backdoor and proxy botnet, connecting infected devices to command-and-control servers to intercept 2FA codes, install further malware, and route malicious traffic through residential IP addresses.

### avalanche-andromeda
avalanche-andromeda denotes activity of the Andromeda botnet (also known as Gamarue) that was hosted or supported by the Avalanche criminal infrastructure. This label tracks Andromeda's use of Avalanche's fast-flux and proxy services for command-and-control resilience before the infrastructure's takedown.

### android.vo1d2
android.vo1d2 (likely "Void 2") is a variant or tracking label for the vo1d malware family, which targets Android-based TV boxes and set-top boxes. Reports indicate it compromises devices to build a botnet (often exceeding 1 million nodes) used for unauthorized activities, potentially involving ad fraud or traffic proxying.

### bumblebee
BumbleBee (win.bumblebee) is a sophisticated malware loader that emerged as a replacement for BazarLoader. It is used by initial access brokers to deliver follow-on payloads such as Cobalt Strike beacons, often leading to ransomware deployments, and is characterized by its heavy anti-analysis and anti-virtualization techniques.

### rhadamanthys
Rhadamanthys (win.rhadamanthys) is an information stealer sold as malware-as-a-service. It is designed to extract a wide range of sensitive data from infected machines, including browser credentials, cryptocurrency wallets, and session cookies, often distributed via malvertising and fake software downloads.

### android.triada
Triada (apk.triada) is a modular trojan for Android that often comes pre-installed on budget devices or infects the Zygote parent process to gain persistence. It is capable of stealing sensitive data (including financial information), modifying app behavior, and downloading additional modules for spam or ad fraud.

### android.vo1d
vo1d (apk.vo1d) is an Android malware family specifically targeting Android TV boxes and set-top boxes. It creates a botnet of infected devices (millions observed) and is capable of downloading and executing arbitrary code, often used for monetization through fraud or proxy services.

### android.hummer
Android.Hummer is a family of aggressive Android trojans (often labeled as adware/downloaders) known for rooting infected devices to gain administrative privileges, installing unwanted applications in the background, and displaying intrusive advertisements to generate revenue.

### avalanche-generic
avalanche-generic is a placeholder or fallback label used to categorize malicious activity or infrastructure associated with the Avalanche botnet ecosystem when a specific malware family cannot be definitively attributed, or when the activity relates to the general management services of the network.

### mirai
Mirai (elf.mirai) is a notorious malware family targeting Linux-based IoT devices (cameras, routers) by exploiting weak default credentials. It aggregates infected devices into massive botnets used primarily for high-volume Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks.

### pseudomanuscrypt
PseudoManuscrypt is a malware family often disguised as pirated software or cracks, which exhibits spyware and sometimes ransomware-like capabilities. It has been observed targeting industrial and government organizations, utilizing complex obfuscation distinct from the related "Manuscrypt" APT tools.

### expiro
Expiro (win.expiro) is a file-infecting virus (polymorphic file infector) that appends its code to executable files (EXE, DLL) on Windows systems. Beyond local infection, it steals system information and user credentials, and can download additional payloads.

### avalanche-ranbyus
avalanche-ranbyus refers to the operation of the Ranbyus (aka Corka) banking trojan within the Avalanche infrastructure. It highlights the use of Avalanche's hosting and proxying services to facilitate Ranbyus's command-and-control communications and campaign resilience.

### avalanche-tinba
avalanche-tinba denotes the specific campaigns of the Tinba ("Tiny Banker") trojan that relied on the Avalanche botnet infrastructure. This association allowed Tinba operators to utilize fast-flux networks to hide their C2 servers while conducting web-injection attacks.

### m0yv
m0yv (win.m0yv) is a modular file infector for x86/x64 Windows systems, linked to the developers of the Maze ransomware. It is capable of infecting executables and has been used to deploy payloads, sometimes being misidentified by antivirus vendors as Expiro.

### sality
Sality (win.sality) is a long-standing and highly resilient polymorphic file infector and botnet agent. It infects executable files, spreads via removable drives and network shares, and creates a peer-to-peer botnet for downloading additional malware and spamming.

### andromeda
Andromeda (win.andromeda, aka Gamarue) is a modular botnet and downloader widely sold in the underground market. It serves as a platform for distributing other malware families (such as ransomware or banking trojans) and includes plugins for keylogging and socks proxying.

### qsnatch
QSnatch (elf.qsnatch) is a malware family specifically targeting QNAP NAS (Network Attached Storage) devices. It modifies the device firmware to gain persistence, prevents updates, and steals user credentials and system configuration data.

### avalanche-nymaim
avalanche-nymaim refers to the Nymaim malware campaigns that were supported by the Avalanche infrastructure. Nymaim is a downloader known for delivering ransomware and banking trojans, and it utilized Avalanche's services for robust C2 communication.

### tinba
Tinba (win.tinba, aka Tiny Banker) is one of the smallest known banking trojans. It hooks into browser processes to perform web injections and man-in-the-browser attacks, stealing login credentials and financial data from infected users.

### non-authoritative-whois
This is likely an operational tag rather than a malware family. It typically refers to WHOIS record data that is not sourced directly from the authoritative registrar, often used in threat intelligence feeds to indicate lower confidence or cached domain registration information.

### necurs
Necurs (win.necurs) is a kernel-mode rootkit and powerful botnet used primarily for mass spam distribution (e.g., pump-and-dump stocks, ransomware delivery). It is known for its resilience and ability to disable security software on infected hosts.

### ranbyus
Ranbyus (win.ranbyus, aka Corka) is a banking trojan designed to steal financial information. It uses web injects to modify banking pages, bypasses smart card authentication in some variants, and monitors network traffic for sensitive data.

### vipersoftx
ViperSoftX (win.vipersoftx) is a Windows-based information stealer and RAT (often distributed as a browser extension or via cracked software). It specializes in stealing cryptocurrency by monitoring clipboard content for wallet addresses and extracting browser data.

### adload
AdLoad (osx.adload) is a persistent macOS adware and loader family observed since at least 2017. It installs itself via a fake Flash Player or other deceptive installers, hijacks browser search settings (search/ad injection), and functions as a backdoor to download and execute additional malicious payloads on infected Mac systems.

### socks5systemz
Socks5Systemz (win.socks5_systemz) is a proxy botnet malware distributed via loaders like PrivateLoader and Amadey. It infects victim machines to turn them into residential SOCKS5 proxies (sold as a service), capable of tunneling traffic for illicit activities such as ad fraud, credential stuffing, and anonymization.

### nymaim

It is known for its heavy obfuscation and stealthy delivery mechanisms, often dropped by exploit kits, and serves as a platform to deploy additional payloads like Gozi or Netwire.

### avalanche-corebot
avalanche-corebot denotes the activity of the CoreBot banking trojan facilitated by the Avalanche infrastructure. This association allowed CoreBot operators to leverage the robust C2 network of Avalanche to maintain control over infected hosts and distribute updates or modules.

### corebot
CoreBot (win.corebot) is a modular banking trojan that emerged around 2015, initially appearing as a simple data stealer. It later evolved to include sophisticated web-injection capabilities for stealing banking credentials and a plugin architecture for adding features like VNC and password grabbing.

### apk.trickmo
TrickMo (apk.trickmo) is an advanced Android banking trojan, originally developed as a mobile component for TrickBot to bypass 2FA (SMS interception). It has evolved into a standalone malware family capable of overlay attacks, screen recording, and remote device control for on-device fraud.

### avalanche-matsnu
avalanche-matsnu refers to campaigns of the Matsnu malware (often a spam bot or downloader) that utilized the Avalanche botnet infrastructure. This integration provided Matsnu with resilient command-and-control channels protected by Avalanche's fast-flux techniques.

### avalanche-kins
avalanche-kins describes the operation of the KINS banking trojan (a Zeus derivative) within the Avalanche ecosystem. KINS operators used Avalanche's services to host their C2 panels and manage the botnet, benefiting from the infrastructure's high availability and resistance to takedowns.

### smokeloader
SmokeLoader (win.smokeloader, aka Dofoil) is a modular malware loader sold on underground forums. Its primary purpose is to download and execute other malware (payloads) on infected systems, often using complex anti-analysis and anti-debugging techniques to evade detection.

### ngioweb
Ngioweb (win.ngioweb, elf.ngioweb) is a proxy malware family that targets both Windows and Linux (IoT) devices. It transforms infected hosts into rotating residential proxies (often part of the NSOCKS botnet) used to route malicious traffic, perform credential stuffing, or support other cybercriminal operations.

### pykspa
Pykspa (win.pykspa) is a worm and backdoor family that spreads primarily via Skype and removable drives. It is designed to steal sensitive information, spread itself to contacts, and can download additional malware, often manipulating browser settings or redirecting traffic.

### rovnix
Rovnix (win.rovnix) is a stealthy bootkit and banking trojan known for infecting the Volume Boot Record (VBR) to gain persistence before the OS loads. It is used to steal financial information via web injections and can load unsigned drivers to bypass Windows security features.

### android.bakdoor.prizmes
Android.Backdoor.Prizmes is a mobile malware family that functions as a backdoor on Android devices. It typically provides attackers with remote control capabilities, allowing them to steal data, install additional apps, or intercept communications without the user's knowledge.

### matsnu
Matsnu (win.matsnu) is a malware family often functioning as a spam bot and downloader. It is characterized by its use of domain generation algorithms (DGA) and has been observed dropping other malware or being used in pay-per-install schemes, heavily relying on botnet infrastructures like Avalanche.

### android.rootnik
Rootnik (apk.rootnik) is an Android malware family that exploits system vulnerabilities (such as the "Dirty COW" exploit) to gain root privileges on infected devices. Once rooted, it installs persistent adware, steals private data, and can silently install other malicious applications.

### kins
KINS (win.kins) is a professional-grade banking trojan toolkit based on the leaked Zeus source code (specifically ZeusVM/Citadel concepts). It features a modular architecture (supporting plugins) and advanced web-injection capabilities to steal banking credentials from infected users.

### likely-rat-adwind

### avalanche-xswkit

### avalanche-teslacrypt
avalanche-teslacrypt denotes the distribution or C2 communication of the TeslaCrypt ransomware facilitated by the Avalanche ecosystem. This indicates that TeslaCrypt operators utilized Avalanche's robust network services to manage victim connections and payment sites during their campaigns.

### sunburst
Sunburst (win.sunburst) is the sophisticated backdoor trojan inserted into the SolarWinds Orion Platform software supply chain (Solarigate). It remained dormant for a period before communicating with C2 servers, allowing the threat actor (APT29/Nobelium) to execute commands and move laterally within high-value target networks.

### flubot-doh
flubot-doh refers to a specific variant or communication method of the FluBot (apk.flubot) Android malware that utilizes DNS over HTTPS (DoH) for command-and-control. This technique encrypts DNS queries to hide C2 lookups from network monitoring and blocking mechanisms.

### avalanche-urlzone
avalanche-urlzone describes the operation of the URLZone (aka Bebloh) banking trojan within the Avalanche infrastructure. URLZone is known for its sophisticated logic to calculate "safe" theft amounts to avoid detection, and it relied on Avalanche for reliable C2 hosting.

### vpnfilter_stage3
vpnfilter_stage3 refers to the third stage modules of the modular VPNFilter malware (elf.vpnfilter) targeting IoT/network devices. These stage 3 plugins provide specific advanced capabilities, such as protocol sniffing (modbus, packet sniffer), traffic manipulation, or destructive payload delivery.

### avalanche-bolek
avalanche-bolek denotes the activity of the Bolek banking trojan/ransomware (a successor to Carberp/Zeus) supported by the Avalanche infrastructure. It indicates that Bolek campaigns leveraged Avalanche's fast-flux proxy network to protect their command-and-control servers from takedown efforts.

### 911-socks5-proxy
911-socks5-proxy refers to the operational activity or malware infrastructure associated with the "911.re" proxy service. This service built a residential proxy network by infecting computers with malware (often delivered via pay-per-install schemes) that converted victim machines into SOCKS5 proxies used for anonymizing cybercriminal traffic.

### avalanche-pandabanker
avalanche-pandabanker denotes campaigns of the PandaBanker malware that were supported by the Avalanche criminal infrastructure. PandaBanker, a Zeus-based banking trojan, leveraged Avalanche's fast-flux network to host its command-and-control servers, ensuring resilience against takedowns.

### ramnit
Ramnit (win.ramnit) is a prominent banking trojan and worm that spreads by infecting executable files (HTML, EXE, DLL) and via removable drives. It is designed to steal sensitive data such as banking credentials, FTP passwords, and browser cookies, often forming large peer-to-peer botnets.

### amadey
Amadey (win.amadey) is a simple but widely used malware loader (bot) sold on Russian-speaking underground forums. Its primary function is to perform system reconnaissance and download additional payloads (stealers, ransomware) onto infected machines, often serving as a first-stage infection vector.

### smokeloader-possible

### avalanche-marcher
avalanche-marcher refers to the Android banking trojan Marcher (android.marcher) when its infrastructure was hosted or proxied by the Avalanche botnet. This association provided Marcher operators with reliable hosting for their C2 panels and phishing overlays targeting mobile banking users.

### stantinko
Stantinko (win.stantinko, elf.stantinko) is a massive botnet operation affecting hundreds of thousands of Windows and Linux systems. It is primarily monetized through adware (browser extensions), click fraud, and cryptocurrency mining, known for its ability to persist on networks for years.

### sality2
Sality2 refers to the modern, evolved variants of the Sality malware family (e.g., Sality.AE and later). These versions feature advanced peer-to-peer (P2P) communication for botnet resilience, polymorphic file infection capabilities, and modules for spamming and downloading other malware.

### xswkit

### bolek
Bolek (win.bolek) is a sophisticated banking trojan and information stealer that shares code similarities with Carberp and Zeus. It features advanced capabilities like web injection, traffic interception, keylogging, and a unique file system for storing config/plugins, and can also act as a worm.

### likely-rat-remcos

### shiz
Shiz (win.shiz, aka Simda) is a complex malware family functioning as a backdoor and banking trojan. It is known for its capability to infect files, modify host files for traffic redirection (pharming), and serve as a distribution platform (pay-per-install) for other malware.

### latrodectus
Latrodectus (win.latrodectus) is a malware loader that emerged as a successor to IcedID. It is designed to download and execute payloads (such as Cobalt Strike) and is typically distributed via email campaigns, acting as a gateway for initial access brokers to compromise networks.

### likely-rat-netwire

### teslacrypt

### win.lumma

### pandabanker

SpyEye (win.spyeye) is a historic banking trojan that competed with and later merged features with Zeus. It included "Kill Zeus" functionality to remove rival bots and offered a user-friendly toolkit for stealing banking credentials via form grabbing and web injects.

### citadel
Citadel (win.citadel) is a highly sophisticated derivative of the Zeus banking trojan. It introduced a collaborative development model (Citadel Store), advanced encryption (AES), and features like video capture and ransomware modules, causing significant financial losses worldwide.

### android.iop
Android.IoP (apk.iop) is a modular Android malware family that roots infected devices to gain system-level privileges. It then installs other malware, displays intrusive ads, and can subscribe users to premium services, often making the device unusable.

### smartapp
smartapp (often associated with avalanche-smartapp) refers to a category of malicious or fraudulent applications (often adware or fake utility apps) distributed via the Avalanche infrastructure, used to generate revenue through ads or misleading subscriptions.

### gazavat
Gazavat (win.gazavat, often detected as Expiro or linked to DMSniff) is a multi-functional backdoor and file infector. It possesses capabilities for DDoS attacks, proxying traffic, loading additional plugins (like hash crackers), and spreading by infecting executable files.

### zeus
Zeus (win.zeus, aka Zbot) is the most iconic banking trojan family, which established the "standard" for financial malware. It uses web injects to steal credentials and a P2P architecture for resilience. Its leaked source code is the foundation for countless modern banking trojan variants.

### downadup
Downadup (aka Conficker, win.conficker) is a massive computer worm that emerged in 2008. It exploited a Windows vulnerability (MS08-067) and used dictionary attacks to infect millions of computers, creating a giant botnet capable of blocking security updates and downloading further malware.

### boaxxe
Boaxxe (win.boaxxe, aka Miuref) is a malware family functioning primarily as a click-fraud bot and downloader. It often arrives via infected email attachments or drive-by downloads, infecting the system to hijack browser traffic and generate fraudulent ad revenue, sometimes downloading additional payloads.

### sharkbot
SharkBot (apk.sharkbot) is an advanced Android banking trojan that uses Automatic Transfer Systems (ATS) to initiate fraudulent money transfers from infected devices without user intervention. It targets banking and cryptocurrency apps, stealing credentials via overlays and intercepting SMS for 2FA bypass.

### padcrypt
PadCrypt (win.padcrypt) is a ransomware family known for being one of the first to offer a live chat support feature for victims. It encrypts files and demands a Bitcoin ransom, distributed mainly via spam emails containing malicious PDF or ZIP attachments.

### android.uupay
Android.Uupay (or the "Uupay" adware/malware library) refers to a pre-installed malicious SDK or adware found in the firmware of some low-cost Android devices. It collects user data, displays intrusive pop-up ads, and can stealthily download and install other applications.

### android.yunclound
Android.Yunclound (likely a reference to "Yun.Cloud" or similar adware/PUP) denotes a family of potentially unwanted programs or malware on Android that aggressively displays advertisements and may collect device information or install further unwanted software.

### enfal-apt
Enfal (win.enfal, aka Lurid) is a long-standing malware family used primarily by Chinese-speaking APT groups (e.g., APT27, Emissary Panda). It functions as a backdoor and downloader, capable of executing commands, managing files, and maintaining persistence on victim networks for espionage.

### dofoil
Dofoil (win.dofoil, aka SmokeLoader) is a modular malware loader. While primarily used to download other malware (like coin miners or ransomware), it features complex anti-analysis techniques to evade detection and persistence mechanisms to remain on the infected system.

### dresscode
DressCode (apk.dresscode) is an Android malware family found in many apps on the Google Play Store. It creates a botnet by establishing SOCKS proxies on infected devices, allowing attackers to tunnel traffic into internal networks that the device is connected to.

### calypso
Calypso (win.calypso) is a Remote Access Trojan (RAT) associated with the threat actor group APT27 (Emissary Panda). It is used for cyber-espionage, enabling attackers to remotely control infected machines, exfiltrate data, and move laterally within compromised networks.

### tsifiri
Tsifiri (or Tsifiri Stealer) is an information-stealing malware family. It typically targets browser data, credentials, and cryptocurrency wallets from infected Windows systems, often distributed via spam or malicious downloads.

### vextrio-help-tds
vextrio-help-tds refers to the VexTrio Traffic Direction System (TDS), a massive malicious traffic distribution network. It redirects users from compromised websites to various malicious content, including scam pages, malware downloads, and phishing sites, operating as a service for other cybercriminals.

### nymaim2
Nymaim2 refers to evolved variants of the Nymaim malware family (win.nymaim). Originally a ransomware/locker, newer versions (Nymaim 2.0 / Goznym) integrated code from the Gozi banking trojan, transforming it into a powerful hybrid threat capable of both locking screens and stealing banking credentials.

### squidoor
Squidoor (osx.squidoor) is a backdoor Trojan targeting macOS systems. It is believed to be associated with Chinese cyber-espionage campaigns (possibly APT22), used to maintain persistent access to infected Mac computers in targeted organizations.

### vinself
Vinself (win.vinself) is a malware family, often described as a backdoor or trojan, linked to the "Surtr" or "Cledd" malware families used by the APT group "Putter Panda" (APT2). It facilitates remote control and data theft from compromised networks.

### proslikefan
Proslikefan (win.proslikefan, aka Wipbot/Turla) is a backdoor used by the Turla APT group. It serves as a reconnaissance tool and simple backdoor to confirm interesting targets before downloading more complex malware modules like the Carbon framework.

### dltminer
Dltminer (win.dltminer) is a cryptocurrency mining malware family. It infects systems to utilize their CPU/GPU resources for mining digital currencies (cryptojacking), often spreading via exploits or bundled with pirated software.

### apk.coper
Coper (apk.coper) is an Android banking trojan that targets banking applications worldwide. It uses overlay attacks to steal credentials, intercepts SMS messages to bypass 2FA, and employs advanced self-defense mechanisms to prevent removal.

### beebone
Beebone (win.beebone, aka Vobfus/Changeup) is a polymorphic downloader worm. It spreads via USB drives and network shares, constantly changing its file signature to evade antivirus detection, and downloads other malware families onto infected machines.

### jar.strrat
STRRAT (jar.strrat) is a Java-based Remote Access Trojan (RAT). It is known for its ability to steal credentials from browsers and email clients, log keystrokes, and includes a "ransomware" module that renames files (though typically without actual encryption) to extort victims.

### suppobox
Suppobox (win.suppobox) is a backdoor trojan often associated with the threat actor "OceanLotus" (APT32). It is used to maintain persistence on victim networks and facilitate the execution of additional payloads for cyber-espionage operations.

### emotet
Emotet (win.emotet) was one of the most dangerous and versatile malware botnets (often called "the world's most dangerous malware"). Originally a banking trojan, it evolved into a modular infrastructure for distributing other threats like Ryuk ransomware and TrickBot via massive spam campaigns.

### str-rat
STRRAT (jar.strrat) is a RAT written in Java (see jar.strrat above). It is distributed via malicious spam emails and provides attackers with remote control capabilities, including keylogging, password theft, and a fake ransomware feature.

### citrix-cve-2023-3519-campaign
This refers to targeted malware campaigns exploiting the critical remote code execution vulnerability (CVE-2023-3519) in Citrix NetScaler ADC/Gateway appliances. Attackers used this exploit to drop web shells and backdoors on unpatched edge devices to gain initial access to corporate networks.

### toxicpanda
ToxicPanda (apk.toxic_panda) is an Android banking RAT and trojan identified in late 2024. It targets banking applications (focusing on Europe and LATAM) to intercept OTPs and perform on-device fraud (ODF), utilizing a custom C2 protocol for communication.

### avalanche-unknown
avalanche-unknown is a generic classification used for malware traffic or binaries associated with the Avalanche infrastructure that could not be definitively attributed to a specific known family at the time of analysis (e.g., unrecognized variants or new payloads).
